# Product Brief: AI Note Helper

## One-sentence idea

An AI note tool that turns messy thoughts into a title, a short summary, and three next actions.

## Who it is for

- Primary user: people who capture ideas quickly on their phone or computer.
- What they do today: save thoughts in Notes, chat apps, Notion, or random documents, then rarely organize them.
- Why they care: scattered thoughts make it hard to know what to do next.

## Pain to solve

- Painful moment: the user has a long messy note but cannot turn it into action.
- Current friction: the user must manually find the title, summary, and next steps.
- What the user no longer needs to do: dig through raw text to find the useful parts.

## First-version goal

The first version proves one thing: after the user enters a messy note, they can get a copyable title, summary, and three next actions.

## Success criteria

- User can complete: create a note, organize it, view the result, and copy the result.
- Proof we can observe: local demo video, core-flow screenshots, automated tests, or manual acceptance notes.
- What does not count as success: input-only UI, uncopyable output, or no useful error message when AI fails.

## Risks and boundaries

- Must not promise: AI output is not guaranteed to be correct and must not make decisions for the user.
- Must not collect: contacts, location, health data, payment data, or unrelated personal data.
- Actions that affect users or money: launch, real payments, real account data, or sending real notes to a third-party service need human confirmation.
