# Product Brief: AI Note Helper

## One-sentence idea

Turn a messy note into a title, a short summary, and three next actions.

## Who it is for

- Primary user: people who capture ideas quickly on their phone or computer.
- What they do today: drop ideas into Notes, chat apps, Notion, or random documents, then rarely come back to them.
- Why they care: scattered notes make it hard to see the next step.

## Pain to solve

- Painful moment: the user writes a long note, closes it, and never turns it into action.
- Current friction: the user still has to pull out the title, summary, and next steps by hand.
- What the user no longer needs to do: dig through raw text to find the useful parts.

## First-version goal

The first version should prove one thing: after the user enters a messy note, they can get a copyable title, summary, and three next actions.

## Success criteria

- User can complete: create a note, organize it, view the result, and copy the result.
- Proof we can observe: local demo video, core-flow screenshots, automated tests, or manual acceptance notes.
- What does not count as success: input-only UI, uncopyable output, or no useful error message when AI fails.

## Risks and boundaries

- Must not promise: the organized result is always correct, or that the product can make decisions for the user.
- Must not collect: contacts, location, health data, payment data, or unrelated personal data.
- Actions that affect users or money: launch, real payments, real account data, or sending real notes to a third-party service need human confirmation.
